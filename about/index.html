I am a Master student at the Institute of Atmospheric and Cryosferic Sciences (ACINN) of the University of Innsbruck. I'm interested in weather, climate and especially snow settlement and avalanche forecasting. For that reason I'm doint a joint Master's thesis between the WSL-Institut für Schnee und Lawinenforschung (SLF) at Davos and the ACINN.

I was born in L'Aquila, a small city in central Italy. I studied Physics at the Department of Physical and Chemical Sciences at the University of L'Aquila. I've been loving mountains since I was a child, especially the Apennines. Snow landscapes always fascinated me and aroused the interest to understand the processes that regulate snowfall, snow settlement and avalanches triggering. In central Italy extreme snowfall are becoming much more frequent during the recent years due to the climate change, and much more people are approaching skitouring and freeride. Nevertheless snow microphysics and avalanches forecasting studies are completely neglected in this region. I hope to bring attention to these topics and contribute with my knowledge.

I'm currently working with MeteoAQuilano, a team that monitors the climate of L'Aquila and the surroundings with a huge number of weather stations and makes daily weather forecasts for these territories with professionalism and passion.
